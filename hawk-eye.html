Hawk-Eye Innovations

A placement year as UX/UI Designer at Hawk-Eye Innovations — designing operator-facing tools for live professional sports operations across basketball, cricket, and tennis.

Role UX / UI Designer
Company Hawk-Eye Innovations
Year 2026
Tools Figma, Miro
Overview

Designing for the world's biggest sporting moments.

During my placement year at Hawk-Eye Innovations, a global sports technology company, I worked as a UX/UI Designer — helping design digital products used in professional sports, turning complex data and tracking systems into clear and easy-to-use interfaces.

I worked closely with product managers and developers to understand user needs, create wireframes and prototypes, and improve designs through testing and feedback. This experience helped me learn how to design practical, user-focused solutions while working within real technical and business constraints.

Project 01

Basketball Status API Feed Tool

A tool that allows operators to manage the status of live basketball games and report data issues in real time. The challenge was designing an interface that supports quick updates while minimising disruption to ongoing live operations.

Prioritising Game Visibility

Operators often need to monitor multiple live games simultaneously. I chose a card-based game list that surfaces status information at a glance, allowing users to quickly identify which games require attention without opening individual views.

Keeping Users in Context

Rather than navigating away from the game list, status updates and error reporting are handled within a modal. This allows operators to complete tasks while maintaining awareness of the wider schedule.

Separating Operational Tasks

Updating game status and reporting issues serve different user goals, so they were separated into dedicated tabs. This reduces cognitive load and helps users focus on the task at hand during time-sensitive situations.

Providing Clear Feedback

Because changes can affect downstream systems, it was important that users understood when actions had been completed successfully. Confirmation messages were included to provide confidence that updates had been processed.

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of designing for efficiency in operational environments. Small decisions such as keeping users in context and reducing navigation can have a significant impact when users are managing multiple live events simultaneously.

Project 02

Cricket "No Ball" Review Panel

As part of the existing cricket review system, I designed a dedicated panel for processing no-ball reviews during live matches. Operators needed to complete a series of decisions before submitting the replay — selecting the delivery, identifying the no-ball type, choosing the batter, and confirming the original umpire decision. The primary challenge was ensuring the process remained simple and reliable under live broadcast conditions.

Guiding Users Through a Fixed Workflow

The review process follows a strict sequence of steps, so the interface was designed around a numbered workflow. This helps operators understand what information is required and reduces the risk of missing critical inputs before submitting a review.

Progressive Validation

Not every outcome can be calculated immediately. Validation and warning states were introduced to highlight missing or incomplete information, allowing issues to be resolved before a replay is sent.

Reducing Decision Complexity

Rather than presenting all available options at once, related actions were grouped into individual stages — trajectory selection, no-ball type, batter selection, and original decision. This keeps attention focused on a single task at a time.

Maintaining Consistency with Existing Tools

As the panel was being integrated into an established review product, the design followed existing interaction patterns and visual conventions. This reduced training requirements and helped operators adopt the new workflow quickly.

Outcome

The final design was incorporated into the cricket review workflow and has since been deployed in the IPL (Indian Premier League).

Project 03

Tennis Logging Tool

A tablet-based logging tool for coaches and players at training facilities. The product needed to support session setup, player configuration, drill selection, and live tracking — while remaining simple enough to use courtside during an active practice session.

Separating Setup from Live Tracking

The experience was split into two phases: session configuration and active drill tracking. This allowed users to focus on entering required information upfront before moving into a simplified tracking interface during practice.

Guiding Users Through Session Creation

Creating a session required multiple pieces of information — players, rackets, drill type, and court positioning. A step-by-step workflow was introduced to reduce complexity and help users complete setup in a logical order.

Designing for Tablet Use

As the tool was intended for courtside use, interactions were designed with large touch targets and clear primary actions. This helps users operate the system quickly without needing precise input.

Emphasising Critical Actions During Tracking

Once a drill begins, the interface shifts focus to the most important actions: starting, stopping, and ending tracking. Secondary controls remain available but are visually de-emphasised to reduce distraction during live sessions.

Outcome

The final design provided a structured workflow for configuring training sessions while keeping the active tracking experience focused and easy to operate in a fast-paced coaching environment.
