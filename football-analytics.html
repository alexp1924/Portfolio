Football Analytics App

A mobile-first prototype designed to help casual football fans understand advanced metrics like expected goals (xG) — through clear visual hierarchy, contextual explanations, and intuitive navigation.

Role UX Designer
Type Final Year Project
Year 2026
Tools Figma, Miro, Google Forms
Overview

Making football data understandable for everyone.

Football fans now have access to more statistics than ever before through platforms such as SofaScore, FotMob, and broadcast graphics. However, advanced metrics such as expected goals (xG) are often difficult for casual fans to understand due to cluttered layouts, limited explanations, and overwhelming amounts of information.

This project explored how UX/UI design could improve football fans' understanding and overall experience of football data, guided by three core principles.

Visual Hierarchy

Making the most important information stand out clearly at a glance.

Contextual Support

Providing in-context explanations for complex statistics like xG.

Usability

Ensuring the interface was intuitive and easy for any fan to navigate.

The Challenge

Built for analysts, not fans.

Many football data platforms are built for experienced users and analysts. Casual fans can struggle to:

  • Understand advanced metrics such as xG.
  • Find the most relevant information quickly.
  • Navigate large volumes of data.
  • Compare players and teams effectively.

The challenge was to design an interface that made football data more accessible, engaging, and easier to understand.

My Role

End-to-end ownership across research, design, and testing.

I led this project in its entirety, covering every stage of the design process.

  • User research and competitor analysis
  • Information architecture
  • Wire-framing and low-fidelity concepts
  • High-fidelity UI design in Figma
  • Interactive prototyping
  • User testing and iteration
Process

Following the Double Diamond from discovery to delivery.

The project followed the Double Diamond design framework — diverging to understand the problem space, then converging on a focused solution before expanding again through development and testing.

01

Discover

Understand how football fans currently consume data and identify common frustrations.

Activities

  • Literature review
  • Competitor analysis — SofaScore, FotMob, Flashscore
  • Online survey with 50 respondents

Key Findings

  • Mobile phones were the most common device used
  • Users were most interested in player statistics
  • Many fans struggled to understand advanced metrics
  • Visualisations were seen as helpful for understanding data
02

Define

Turn research findings into clear design requirements and user needs.

User Requirements

  • Show key player statistics prominently
  • Provide explanations for advanced metrics
  • Use visualisations to support understanding
  • Allow filtering by league and season
  • Enable switching between player and team statistics
  • Keep the layout simple and uncluttered

Design Principles

  • Visual Hierarchy
  • Contextual Support
  • Usability
03

Develop

Design and prototype a solution that addresses the identified user needs.

Key Features

  • Personalised dashboard with favourite teams
  • Match centre with overview, lineups, statistics, and standings
  • Player and team statistics pages
  • Contextual help pop-ups for metrics such as xG
  • Player comparison tools
  • Heatmaps and form guides

Design Decisions

  • Grouped content cards to reduce cognitive load
  • Strong visual hierarchy to highlight key information
  • Segmented controls and bottom navigation for intuitive browsing
  • On-demand explanations to support understanding of complex metrics
04

Deliver

Evaluate whether the prototype improved understanding and overall user experience.

Testing Method

  • Task-based activities — finding a match, accessing player info, interpreting a statistic
  • Post-test questionnaire measuring usability, understanding, and visual hierarchy

Key Learning

  • Simple navigation and contextual explanations received the most positive feedback
  • UX/UI design can significantly improve how football data is understood
Usability Testing Results

5.0/5

Ease of use — all participants completed all tasks successfully

4.75/5

Navigation clarity and overall information architecture

4.5/5

Visual hierarchy across screens and data presentations

4.25/5

Contextual explanations for complex metrics including xG

Outcome

Complex data, made genuinely accessible.

The final prototype demonstrated how football analytics can be presented in a way that is easier to understand, more visually engaging, simpler to navigate, and better suited to a wider range of fans.

This project reinforced the importance of combining research with design decisions. By focusing on visual hierarchy, contextual support, and usability, complex football data was transformed into a more accessible and enjoyable experience.

Future improvements could include live data integration, greater personalisation, and testing with a larger and more diverse audience.
