Smart Boot Companion App

A mobile companion app for smart football boots — guiding users through device setup, live session tracking, and post-session analysis while keeping performance insights accessible to non-professional athletes.

Role UX / UI Designer
Type Mobile App Design
Year 2026
Tools Figma, Miro
The Challenge

Making performance data accessible to every player.

I designed a mobile companion app for smart football boots that capture performance data during training sessions. The app needed to guide users through device setup, live session tracking, and post-session analysis while keeping performance insights accessible to non-professional athletes.

Primary Users

Two audiences with different — but connected — needs.

01

Amateur Football Players (18–25)

Goals

  • Improve performance over time
  • Understand training intensity
  • Measure progress objectively
  • Stay motivated through measurable improvements

Pain Points

  • Limited access to performance analytics
  • Difficulty tracking progress consistently
  • Reliance on subjective coaching feedback
  • Unclear understanding of strengths and weaknesses
02

Coaches

Goals

  • Monitor player workload
  • Compare performance between sessions
  • Identify fatigue or performance drops
  • Support training planning
Competitor Analysis

Understanding where existing solutions fall short.

To understand the market, I reviewed existing football tracking solutions including Playermaker and Statsports Apex Athlete. While both offer strong data capabilities, a consistent pattern emerged: they focus on presenting data rather than helping users understand what to do with it.

This became the central design opportunity — helping players answer three questions: What happened? Why does it matter? What should I improve next?

Strengths

  • Detailed physical and technical metrics
  • Session history and benchmarking
  • Strong credibility within football environments

Weaknesses

  • Large amounts of data can feel overwhelming
  • Initial device setup can be complex
  • Insights often require interpretation rather than clear recommendations
Key Design Decisions

Four principles that shaped the experience.

Reducing Setup Friction

Connecting hardware can be a major source of user drop-off, so onboarding was designed as a guided step-by-step process. Users are progressively introduced to Bluetooth permissions, device discovery, and boot pairing, making the setup process feel approachable.

Focusing on Meaningful Metrics

Rather than overwhelming users with raw sensor data, the dashboard prioritises a small number of performance indicators such as touches, kicks, intensity, and distance covered. This helps users quickly understand their performance without needing specialist knowledge.

Supporting Both Live and Historical Analysis

The app was designed around two distinct use cases: tracking a live training session and reviewing long-term progress. Live screens focus on session control and real-time feedback, while post-session screens emphasise trends and comparisons across multiple sessions.

Encouraging Performance Improvement

Comparison charts were included to help players identify patterns and track progress over time. The goal was not simply to display data, but to make performance improvements visible and motivating.

Outcome

A complete player journey from first boot to first improvement.
